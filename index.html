Who he is

About

Four years of Division I football taught me how to prepare, compete, and finish. I bring the same discipline to investing.

I’m an MBA candidate at Washington University in St. Louis, Olin Business School, where I study finance and strategy as a Toigo LIFE Fellow and Consortium scholar. Before Olin, I analyzed deals at Indigo Partners, the Phoenix private equity firm W. A. Franke founded in 2002 to invest in air transportation.

I learned business hands-on first. A three-year property management apprenticeship taught me tenant relations, budgeting, and how to fix what breaks. At Northern Arizona University, I played D1 football, served as president of the Investment Club, and researched financial markets while earning a BBA in finance and economics. Internships at Charles Schwab and Copper Sky Capital pointed me toward investing.

Outside of work, I volunteer for causes in economic empowerment and workforce development.
